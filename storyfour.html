Mrs. DALLOWAY

AUTHOR: Virginia Woolf

"Mrs. Dalloway" is a novel by Virginia Woolf that follows Clarissa Dalloway as she prepares to host a party in London after World War I. The story takes place over the course of a single day, with Woolf employing her signature stream-of-consciousness style to explore the inner lives of Clarissa and other characters. Clarissa, a high-society woman in her 50s, is contrasted with Septimus Warren Smith, a shell-shocked veteran struggling with mental illness. While Clarissa's life is one of luxury and social status, Septimus's is marked by trauma and disconnection. Through the characters' inner monologues and observations, Woolf explores themes of identity, class, trauma, and the human experience. The novel is a powerful commentary on the social and psychological effects of war and the constraints of societal expectations. Ultimately, "Mrs. Dalloway" is a rich and introspective novel that celebrates the beauty of life and the complexity of human emotions, while also acknowledging the darkness and fragility that underlie human experience.
