MISS PETIGREW LIVES FOR A DAY

AUTHOR: Winifred Watson

Here is a summary of "Miss Pettigrew Lives for a Day" by Winifred Watson: "Miss Pettigrew Lives for a Day" is a 1938 novel by Winifred Watson that tells the story of Miss Guinevere Pettigrew, a middle-aged, unmarried governess who has fallen on hard times. The story takes place in London in the 1930s. After being fired from her job, Miss Pettigrew visits a employment agency, where she is mistakenly sent to the apartment of a glamorous nightclub singer, Miss Delysia LaFosse. Over the course of a single day, Miss Pettigrew becomes embroiled in Miss LaFosse's complicated love life and helps her navigate a series of romantic entanglements. Through her adventures with Miss LaFosse, Miss Pettigrew undergoes a transformation, discovering a newfound sense of confidence, independence, and even romance. As the day comes to a close, Miss Pettigrew reflects on the lessons she has learned and the possibilities that lie ahead. The novel is a charming, witty, and insightful exploration of female friendship, love, and self-discovery, with a strong focus on the social and economic realities faced by women during the 1930s.
