CONVENIENCE STORE WOMAN

AUTHOR: Sayaka murata

"Convenience Store Woman" is a novel by Sayaka Murata that tells the story of Keiko Furukura, a 36-year-old Japanese woman who has worked at a convenience store for 18 years. The novel explores themes of societal expectations, individuality, and happiness. Keiko's life is simple and routine, and she finds comfort in the predictable nature of her job. However, her family and society pressure her to conform to traditional norms, such as getting married and having children. The novel delves into Keiko's inner world, revealing her unique perspective on life and her struggles to navigate societal expectations. Through Keiko's story, Murata critiques Japanese societal norms and highlights the challenges faced by women who choose not to conform. "Convenience Store Woman" is a poignant and thought-provoking novel that explores what it means to be happy and fulfilled in a society with rigid expectations.
