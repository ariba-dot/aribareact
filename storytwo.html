THE UNCOMMON READER

AUTHOR: Alan Benette

"The Uncommon Reader" is a novella by Alan Bennett that tells the story of Queen Elizabeth II, who develops a passion for reading after stumbling upon a mobile library. The story begins with the Queen encountering the mobile library while walking her dogs. She borrows a book by Ivy Compton-Burnett and becomes hooked, soon devouring books by authors such as Nancy Mitford and Anthony Powell. As the Queen's reading habit grows, she begins to neglect her royal duties, causing concern among her staff. Her new passion also leads to changes in her relationships with her family and advisors. Through the Queen's newfound love of reading, Bennett explores themes of identity, class, and the British monarchy. The novella is a witty and insightful commentary on the humanizing effects of literature and the transformative power of reading. Ultimately, "The Uncommon Reader" is a charming and humorous tale that reimagines the Queen as a bookworm, highlighting the universal appeal of reading and its ability to bring people together.
